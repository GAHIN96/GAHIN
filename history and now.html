Overview

The Institute of Mathematics, Mechanics and Computer Sciences (Mehmat) is the largest institute of natural sciences in the Southern Federal University — until 2006 it was called the Faculty of Mechanics and Mathematics. The name change is associated with the expansion of the profile of the Institute and the opening in 2005 of the direction of training "Fundamental Informatics and Information Technology". Thus, at present, the institute has a full range of directions and specialties presented in its name:

  • Mathematics (budget admission of 25 people);
  • Applied Mathematics and Computer science (admission of 100 people to the full-time department);
  • Mechanics and mathematical modeling (admission of 25 people);
  • Fundamental computer science and information technology (admission of 50 people to the full-time department and 15 people to the evening department);
  • Information systems and technologies (until 2013, evening department only, admission of 15 people).

Departments and Teaching Staff

About 150 highly qualified teachers work at 15 departments of the faculty. Among them are 40 doctors of sciences, professors, 94 candidates of sciences, associate professors. Among them are Honored Scientists of Russia Yu.F. Korobeynik, Yu.A. Ustinov, Honored Worker of the Higher School of the Russian Federation Ya.M. Yerusalimsky, Soros professors A.O. Vatulyan, L.M. Zubov, S.M. Grudsky, Ya.M. Yerusalimsky, Yu.A. Ustinov, Soros associate professors V.B. Dybin, M.Yu. Zhukov, K.A. Nadolin, V.G. Tsybulin, Yu.V. Tyutyunov, B.Ya. Steinberg.
